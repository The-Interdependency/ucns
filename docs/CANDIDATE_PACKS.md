# UCNS first evaluator candidate packs

**Status:** explicit noncanonical candidates for laboratory pressure.  
**Consumers:** internal UCNS research only.

## Equivalence candidates

Initial constructors include:

1. exact evidence digest through a named content adapter;
2. digest after a named structural-policy projection of one retained layer;
3. digest over explicitly selected retained-layer names.

These candidates produce signatures. Equal signatures are candidate-equivalence outputs under the declared adapter and scope, not canonical equivalence.

## Product-character candidates

The first cell-only candidates are:

- geometric mean of positive cell supports;
- maximum positive cell support;
- minimum positive cell support.

All three are multiplicative under the established Cartesian cell pairing law for positive-support carriers. They remain competing candidates and must still meet null, separation, scope, holdout, and counterexample obligations.

A cell-only candidate fails outside scope when it receives retained evidence without a cell carrier. It does not treat unmeasured layers as absent distinction.

## Faithful-breadth candidates

Initial constructors include:

- logarithm of aggregate cell support;
- cell-detail breadth using support terms and occurrence count;
- retained-presence breadth using cell structure plus retained-layer occurrence presence.

These are pressure candidates. They are expected to expose disagreements and failed sensitivity or invariance laws.

## Decision packets

A candidate decision packet records:

- candidate identity and scope;
- development report;
- holdout report;
- minimized counterexamples;
- alternatives retained;
- declared information loss;
- rollback behavior;
- separate candidate, witness, and decision authorship.

A packet can become reviewable. It cannot make itself canonical.

## Nonclaims

No candidate in this file is canonical `M`, canonical `B`, or canonical structural equivalence. No theorem, arithmetic, factorization, codec, embedding, or downstream status follows from a passing fixture.
