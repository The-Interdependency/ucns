# Operational widening in the normalized factorization model

## Status correction

This document concerns the internal projected carrier `n_min` of normalized
recursive factorization objects. It is not a theorem about widening of the
canonical public gonol.

The earlier document flattened:

```text
public 157-position twist-bearing carrier
internal projected n_min
EDCM/edcmbone token carriers
```

into one carrier concept and described finite probes as proof of an unbounded

## 2. Internal projected `n_min`

For normalized recursive factorization objects, the implementation computes an
internal invariant `n_min` from denominators of projected internal values.

The sorry-free Carrier-LCM theorem establishes, on its declared `Complete`
domain:

```text
nMin(multiplyFuel A B) = lcm(nMin A, nMin B)
```

This supports an internal prime-support statement:

> If the internal `n_min` values of factors use only primes in `S`, their product
> `n_min` also uses only primes in `S`.

That is ordinary LCM arithmetic applied to the internal invariant. It does not
show that `S` is the complete set of public-gonol dimensions, semantic axes, or
orientation states.

---

## 3. What the bounded probes establish

Repository probes may demonstrate selected fixtures such as:

- closure of chosen internal `n_min` values under LCM;
- associativity or order behavior of the tested implementation;
- construction within selected prime-support bands;
- application behavior for exact pinned operands.

An executed probe establishes only its recorded finite cases. It does not prove:

- the universal public-gonol carrier law;
- asymptotic tractability;
- linguistic dimensionality;
- security;
- all-prime or all-depth completeness;
- the identity of public carrier support with internal `n_min` support.

Numerical pass counts require immutable execution artifacts. Source loops alone
are configured cases, not proof or recorded execution.

---

## 4. Operational versus analytic work

Within the internal factorization model:

### Forward construction

If operands and their internal prime support are known, forward multiplication
can compute the product without factoring it afterward.

### Analytic recovery

Recovering factors from a product remains governed by quotient/search
completeness in the declared domain. Theorem N remains `FRONTIER` and
`sorry`-backed.

This distinction may be useful operationally, but it is not a cryptographic
security theorem. Security cannot be based on a claim that factoring is hard or
incomplete unless a separate threat model and proof/evidence establish it.

---

## 5. PCEA/PTCA boundary

PCEA and PTCA may choose prime-indexed internal carriers as application policy.
Such choices are downstream design decisions.

A valid application claim must state:

- exact internal object domain;
- exact chosen prime support;
- key material and threat model;
- whether inversion uses retained operands, keys, quotients, or factor search;
- information exposed by carrier support;
- public-gonol bridge status;
- proof-status non-transfer.

No application may claim that the fixed public-gonol origin or 720-degree return
is preserved merely because its internal `n_min` stays in a prime lattice.

---

## 6. Correct internal law

The defensible law is:

> **Internal projected-support closure.** For normalized factorization objects on
> the domain where Carrier-LCM applies, the prime support of the product's
> internal `n_min` is contained in the union of the operands' internal `n_min`
> prime supports.

This follows from the proved internal LCM equality and ordinary prime support of
LCM.

It is intentionally not called “per-sublattice finiteness of UCNS” because:

- exponents may still grow unless bounded;
- the set of possible values in a prime-generated multiplicative monoid is not
  finite without exponent bounds;
- the public gonol has additional twist/orientation structure;
- application carriers and semantic dimensions are separate.
